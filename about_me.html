It's Anu's World!

Hi, my name is Anubhuti, but I go by Anu! I am a senior at Columbia University in the City of New York studying biochemistry. I am a Science Research Fellow in the Columbia Undergraduate Scholars Program which means that I have made scientific research the cornerstone of my undergraduate experience. I have used my funding to work in a variety of laboratories during the semesters and summers. My experiences have expanded my lab skills and deepened my understanding and appreciation for biological sciences. You can read about my projects here. I am passionate about spreading my love for science through my involvement in the Columbia Science Review publication, which seeks to improve science literacy on campus.

Aside from research, I also serve at the VP Internal for the Columbia University chapter of Girls Who Code, an organization with the mission to close the gender gap in tech. In high school, I had always been fascinated by computer science but didn't have any opportunities to take classes to explore that interest. I envied my male friends that had taught themselves how to code but felt too intimidated to try teaching myself. I finally took my first introductory computer science course in my freshman year of college, but as a biochemistry student, I didn't have much free space in my schedule to explore CS further. Through Girls Who Code at Columbia University, I can ensure that high school girls have access to the education and experiences I wish I had. Building this website is an opportunity for me to apply some of the web development skills I have gained over time and hopefully inspire me to pursue other technical projects.
